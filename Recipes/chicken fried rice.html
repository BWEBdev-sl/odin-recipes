Chicken Fried Rice

Description

Chicken Fried Rice is a popular dish in Sri Lanka, loved for its rich flavors and versatility. Originating from Chinese cuisine, it has been adapted to suit Sri Lankan tastes with spicier ingredients like chili flakes, pepper, and curry leaves. Often served with devilled chicken, chili paste (katta sambol), and vegetable chop suey, it is a favorite meal for both everyday dining and special occasions. Whether enjoyed at restaurants, street food stalls, or home-cooked feasts, Sri Lankans relish their fried rice with bold, aromatic flavors that make it a satisfying and comforting dish.

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • Boneless chicken (cut into small pieces)
  • Eggs
  • Carrots (finely chopped)
  • Spring onions (chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ginger (minced)
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce (optional)
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Chili flakes (optional for spice)
  • Cooking oil
  • Curry leaves (optional, for Sri Lankan flavor)
  • Chili paste (optional, for extra heat)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large wok or pan over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and ginger, stirring until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces, season with salt and pepper, and stir-fry until fully cooked.
  4. Push the chicken to one side of the pan and pour in the beaten eggs.
  5. Scramble the eggs and mix them with the chicken.
  6. Add the chopped carrots and stir-fry for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add the cooked rice and break up any clumps while stirring.
  8. Pour in soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using), mixing well to coat the rice evenly.
  9. Add black pepper, chili flakes, and curry leaves (if using), then mix thoroughly.
  10. Stir in chopped spring onions and give it a final toss.
  11. Serve hot with chili paste or devilled chicken on the side.
