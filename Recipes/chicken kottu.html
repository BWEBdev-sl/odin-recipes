Chicken Kottu

Description

Chicken Kottu is a popular Sri Lankan street food dish made by stir-frying shredded Godhamba roti (a type of flatbread) with chicken, vegetables, eggs, and a blend of aromatic spices. It is often served with a side of spicy curry or gravy for added flavor. Kottu originated in Sri Lanka in the late 20th century as a creative way to use leftover roti, and it has since become a beloved comfort food across the country. The dish is known for its signature rhythmic chopping sound, as vendors skillfully mix and chop the ingredients on a hot griddle using metal blades.

Ingredients

  • Godhamba Roti
  • 250g of Chicken
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 1 cup of carrot
  • 1 cup of cabbage
  • 1 cup of leeks
  • 1 cup of onion
  • few bulbs of garlic
  • chopped green chillies
  • 1 chopped tomato
  • Curry leaves
  • 1 tsp of turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp of chilli powder
  • 1 tsp of curry powder
  • 1 tsp of pepper powder
  • 1 tsp of mustard seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • butter or oil
  • chicken stock or gravy (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the chicken with salt, pepper, and a little curry powder. Once cooked, shred it into small pieces.
  2. Stack the Godhamba roti (or flatbread) and cut it into small strips.
  3. Thinly slice the cabbage, carrots, leeks, and onions. Chop the tomatoes and green chilies.
  4. Heat oil or butter in a large wok or flat pan over medium heat.
  5. Add minced garlic, curry leaves, and green chilies. Stir for a few seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add onions, carrots, leeks, and cabbage. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Mix in the shredded chicken and chopped tomatoes. Sprinkle curry powder, turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Stir well.
  8. Push the ingredients to one side of the pan. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble them. Once cooked, mix them with the rest of the ingredients.
  9. Add the chopped roti strips and mix well to combine everything.
  10. Pour in chicken stock or gravy to keep the kottu moist and flavorful. Stir everything together and let it cook for 2 minutes.
  11. Add soy sauce (if using) and adjust salt or pepper if needed.
  12. Serve hot with extra curry or gravy on the side!
